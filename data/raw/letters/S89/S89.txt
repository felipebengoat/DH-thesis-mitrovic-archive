LUIS MITROVICH,
Casilla 2757,
SANTIAGO DE CHILE.
Santiago, July 3rd, 1935
Professor
Alexander Popp,
Academie der Bildenden Künste,
VII Karl Schweighofergasse 3,
Vienna.
Dear Professor Popp,
I have been duly informed by my son Luis of the kind attention which you have shown him during the first year of his study at the Academy of Vienna, as well as of your invaluable advice that it would be beneficial to his professional study if he returned to Vienna and, what I appreciate more than anything is the fact that you will permit him to practise in your own Offices, under your guidance.
I feel, my dear Professor, I have been most ungrateful to you for not having written you before to express my thanks for the fatherly advices given to my son. I sincerely hope that he will throughout his life remember and appreciate the advice and assistance you have given him and which will be of utmost importance to his future.
My wife and I have no words to express to you our gratitude for the kind attention shown to our son, for which we are deeply indebted and would be glad if we could reciprocate in some way on this side.
Requesting you to convey my greetings to Mrs. Popp and with kindest personal regards, believe me,
Yours sincerely,
L. Mitrovich
