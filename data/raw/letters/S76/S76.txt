Dear Luis,
your letter of 13 October has just reached me via Umwegen (new address, see above!) Your message finds me in great sorrow, for my dear wife Pia, with whom I was happily and harmoniously married for 36 years, passed away a few months ago after a relatively short but serious illness. She died despite chemotherapy from Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (cancer). I am now suddenly alone at 76, my wife was only 66 years old.
But your letter was for me a great joy, an enormous surprise. I had tried several times to reach you by letter in Santiago — how shall I tell you — later — but the letters came back from Chile via the local post. Today I am writing only briefly, so that you know that I still exist and that I have received your dear, very interesting letter. In my files I looked back and saw that your last letter, written on board the MS Dresden, is dated 15.7.37! I will write to you in detail about "my" 50 years, also about John Dijkstra etc. You still write quite excellent German. Lucho, you are a true language genius! As I said, a long letter will follow — for today many heartfelt thanks and friendly, dear greetings from
Rolf Altenburger
