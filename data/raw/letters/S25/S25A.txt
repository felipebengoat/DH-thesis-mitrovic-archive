TELEPHONE CONFERENCE
DATE: 5 January 1977
PARTICIPANTS: Lucho Mitrovic and Lucho Vera, Advisor Tourism Projects Department, World Bank.

L. Vera called L. Mitrovic from Washington D.C., Monday, 4 January 1977, re: Petra and Jerash Development Plan Proposal through IBRD Loan.
Vera said that the Bank had received on January 2, 1977, a cable from the Ministry in Jordan communicating that in a few days they would have the short list of preselected consultants. They wished the Bank would send somebody of their staff that would advise them for the final selection of the consultant to do the design and inspection of the project. For this purpose Lucho Vera himself would arrive in Amman on the 20th of January 1977.
Vera was very impressed with TAC-TUKAN proposal. It very much depends now on the impression that the local consultants working with TAC would make. He furthermore said that the Bank did not like the actual hotel outside the Sik. Also, that they dislike the architecture, specifically the public area, the existing tomb, the building of the bar room in the existing cave. The Bank would demand the pulling down of the building except the hotel rooms that could somehow be connected with a new hotel built in a different site.
