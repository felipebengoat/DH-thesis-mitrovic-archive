24th November, 1936.
Mr. Luis Mitrovich B.,
Legation de Chili,
VIENNA.
Austria.
Dear Lucho,
I was glad to get your letter of the 16th instant and to hear that you had reached Vienna safely, after what must have been for you, an interesting trip abroad.
Your news about Abovich's death was a great shock to me, and I feel very sorry for Franica with her two little boys still to be educated. Abovich always appeared to me to be a very healthy man and a clean liver, and I cannot understand how a man of his type should have died from an attack of angina. My wife and I are sending letters of sympathy to Franica by this post.
I was glad to receive "saludos" from your father, and I feel I did very little for you during your stay here in London. At the same time, I felt with Marta here and other friends, you were able to find your way about.
Regarding this money question, I see my Secretary has explained things to you by letter. I would not let the matter worry you, because I feel that your father will wish you to have sufficient funds to live comfortably. We always send him a note of the payments made to you and the balance of money standing to his credit. He has never written to the firm to cut you down in any way.
I thank you for your offer regarding Agustin Edwards, jun., but, as a matter of fact, I have very little interest today in Chilean affairs, and my work here consists entirely in looking after the London business. At the same time, I shall not forget if I do, at any time, require any private information.
The serious situation in Spain is creating a certain amount of nervousness in this country, but I trust that there will be no war and that matters will gradually adjust themselves.
Trusting this will find you and your friend, Reisser, in the best of health, and,
With kind regards,
Yours sincerely,

SI/WH
