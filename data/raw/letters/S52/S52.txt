Dear Lucho,

How nice to get your letter! Harriet and I both enjoyed hearing from you and especially about your writing your memoirs. We believe they will be very interesting. I once was interviewed about my life by Harriet on tape and had it typewritten afterwards. 240-plus pages but nothing like a written memoir.

It is curious that your drought coincides with that in our country — especially here in the Northeast. We finally had a heavy rain in mid-September, which helped a good deal.

Our political situation is difficult. The Republican Congress is very bad (by our standards), and I hope they are creating problems for themselves in the year 2000 elections. George W. Bush does not appeal to Harriet and me, but our two Democratic candidates, Gore and Bradley, although both good men, are not very good campaign speakers. So we are worried.

I hope your plan to go to Brazil works well, and we would love to join you but cannot do so, alas!

Warm regards and our love,
Harriet and Don
