Stralsund, 10 July 1936.
Dear Luis, I had no opportunity to say goodbye to you before my departure from Weimar. That I am working here on the Baltic Sea during the holiday period I have already told you. Unfortunately I am not allowed to write to you anything about the nature of the assignment area, since the letter is going abroad. The work is very interesting. I have a 70-meter long building to work on completely independently. In this building a district heating system is planned, from which a large number of other buildings will be heated. So very interesting and instructive.
My working hours are fabulous. It starts at 7 in the morning until 3 in the afternoon. Saturdays until 12:30. Afterwards I jump into the Baltic Sea every day. That gives me a great deal of fun. All the Baltic Sea resorts are quickly reachable from here, one can also get to Sweden very cheaply. Beautiful women there are in vast quantities. I go off with a different one almost every day. When one sees these sinful figures in bathing suits daily one cannot do otherwise. It suits me excellently though. My salary is also decent. I no longer need to budget myself as carefully as in Weimar. When you write to Rolf, please give him my regards. I have not seen him either anymore, his address has gotten away from me.
Otherwise I would have nothing new to report to you.
Be warmly greeted
from your Herbert Fronisius
Please write to me how you are getting on with the work in Vienna.
My address:
Herbert Fronisius
Stralsund (Germany)
(Pension Forsch) Heilgeiststr. 61 I
