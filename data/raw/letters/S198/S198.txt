Den Haag, 17 January 1990.
Dear Luis,
From Rolf Altenburger I recently got your address. What a surprise, to hear from you after more than 50 years.
How are you? What have you been doing all these years? Now that it has become a bit more pleasant to visit East-Germany, I may go soon to Weimar and have a look at the "Haus in der Sonne".
Since I left Germany in 1937 I have done a lot of flying. My logbook shows about 16.000 flying hours. During the last world-war I was flying in the British Royal Air Force and at the end of the war I joined KLM and flew for them during 10 years, mainly to Indonesia from Amsterdam out. After that I was in aircraft sales (mainly Cessna's) and I flew a twin-engined Aerocommander for 7 years from Brussels as my home-base.
Now I'm selling Frasca (American) Flight Simulators in all European countries except England, Spain and Italy. In a few weeks time I shall go by car to France, Switzerland and Austria, — all in connection with my business. I intend to continue for at least one more year. I'm now 76, and in good health fortunately. I'm feeling very sorry for Rolf, who lost his wife not so long ago. His health does not seem to be very good at present.
Please excuse me for typing this letter, but I have a very bad handwriting.
I shall be very pleased to hear from you and may be that we might even meet again, be it in Europe or the USA. I doubt if I shall ever go to Chile. What was your latest contact with the Gräfin? I had still one or two letters from her after I had left Germany.
Kind regards,
John
My handwriting is really too bad!
