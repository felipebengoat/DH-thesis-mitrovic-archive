Dipl. Architekten Z.V.
Leo Schmoll — Franz J. Kranl
Wien VII, Neubaugasse 68 / Tel. B-35-6-20
26 Sept. 1936
Dear Luis!
Gratefully received your London letter. How many kilometers has your little "Austin" — the street flea in which you feel like baby elephants — already devoured? How many curbs and trees have you already bent, not to mention the chickens and geese that would have been run over with feathers flying! How did you get on? Here we have had continuously the most beautiful weather, lovelier than in summer. The day before yesterday one could still swim in the Danube without a "whimper."
But Hurrah! Just as I prophesied in early summer — you can after all go to London — and if it were possible, I could still get a Burberry coat this year, ha!
Since it is now beginning to rain I was going to buy one here anyway in the near future — but if it were possible for you to bring me the same one as you have, your size of course — I would be absolutely thrilled. It was my secret wish in spring — hopefully it works out now. I would ask you, if you buy one for me, to write and let me know, so that I can survive the bad weather trembling until your arrival.
I am also already looking forward to the wretched ski and card playing on the glacier with Wynold, who takes his pipe along and then finds sardines and glacier fleas. Stylish!
Now to the Behrens school question. Popp tells me just now by telephone that he has written you a letter.
I was this morning at Tosch's, the most deeply bowed and most respectful government councillor. Well, he doesn't know anything more directly, or doesn't say so. The real decision follows at the first professors' collegium meeting around October 18th.
But the matter is this: the Behrens school stays as and where it is and will not be merged with Holzmeister. Who will lead it will be decided at the above-mentioned date, will probably result in Popp, naturally I don't know more precisely and can't say so! But you should both just come.
If it really were nothing with Popp, hold him to his promise that nothing will stand in the way of taking the diploma examination. (Tuition fees) His equalization with domestic students he naturally cannot promise, that is a matter for the decision of a professors' collegium — but there is nothing standing against a non-refusal of it. So this matter would also go in order — and nothing more stands in the way of a return to Vienna.
Thus spoke the government councillor, and now lets Reiss and Mitrovich greet each other nicely. Send him cards from England, he has a mad delight with the new stamps! Stick exactly 3 different kinds on it!
Hopefully you will soon have England in the bag, haven't run anything over and come back safe and sound, without a Windsor-style full beard.
So much more pleasure for England and North Deutschland and to a reunion in October in Vienna.
All two greetings,
most cordially,
Franz Kranl
