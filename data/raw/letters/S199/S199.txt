Den Haag, 27 March 1993.
Dear Luis,
Thank you very much for your letter of the 9th of this month. I'm always pleased to hear from friends, especially old friends.
What a fine unemployment figure for Chile. It is considerable lower than we have here in Holland. I have high hopes that the world trade motor, the U.S.A., will recover quickly and then all of us should profit. I'm worried about Russia, but the situation there may well have cleared by the time you receive this letter.
My surgeon I see now every fortnight and that, I expect, will still go on for a few months. The radiation treatment I got was very severe, but I'm almost certain, that it killed the cancer.
I'm working quite hard. The results are however rather meagre. This is mainly due to the recession in all the European countries. This coming week I may fly to Lyon to see a prospect. Flying in Europe is quite expensive as you may know.
Thank you for the very nice pictures you send. I have a Minolta Dynax 5000 i camera and a Sony Videocamera. I use them very little. That will no doubt change when I stop working.
It was interesting to hear that you and Angela may visit China and Hongkong. I was in the latter place many years ago and stayed then in the Peninsula Hotel. I remember the huge bathrooms in that famous Hotel, but that may have changed by now.
The weather is here now fine, but the temperature is still "fresh". At the moment I stay rather close to my surgeon, but it may well be, that in a few months time I can go further afield and make use of your kind invitation to come and stay with you and Angela.
My very best wishes to both of you,
Love from
John
