Dear Lucho!
Time passes rapidly and here the tree leaves are already turning yellow and red, it is becoming misty and damp as autumn arrives. Yesterday was Sunday, a magnificent proper autumn day; I was with my friend Kleino in the Odenwald, at his father's large country house. In the morning we also went hunting and a powerful experience was the bellowing of the stag in the dark forest, eerily beautiful this rolling thunderous roaring of a lovesick stag's chest — gripping primeval sounds that one experiences there in the forest. By the way I also secretly shot a roe deer, so that one has something to show for the stalking. Otherwise life is quite good there in Frankfurt, only the work at Roeckle's is not entirely satisfying. In summer we were once more at the car racing at the Nürburgring in the Eifel, at the Ordensburg of the N.S.D.A.P. "Vogelsang", then in Cologne at the exhibition and also once in Düsseldorf at the World Exhibition, also spent a few days in Berlin.
Otto was in Paris at the World Exhibition alone; he likes it now in Hamburg with the Heringens, would like to get back more into the mountains, to southern Germany. Maxi Magazy is active in the Kleines Walsertal, König Paul is working in Berlin; Erich asks what you are up to and why you don't get in touch, he by the way saves every single Kreuzer treats himself to nothing at all — in order to soon buy himself a small Ford, Eifel type.
What are you actually up to? Ha? You never let anyone hear from you — or are you working so much in architecture? One reads by the way: the Chilean state is giving Anita Lizana a house in England — what is that about — is she perhaps even making the design for your Tennis Donna? Yes, you were actually going to write to me sometime!
What is going on with the skiing at Erich Daham's? Your Andes with the powder snow? What else are you always up to?
Where is Tjark and what is he doing?
At home with us in Vienna there is nothing new at all. Everything as before. Here in Germany there was a mighty political wind through the Duce's visit; in London the heads of the Non-Intervention Committee's main committee are smoking from sheer confusion and in Spain one brother is bashing in the other's skull. And in China?
Well then, hopefully a grim earthquake does not shake you too much and that you let yourself be heard from once
with many greetings
from beautiful Old Frankfurt
Servus
Frankfurt, 18 October 1937
Franz Kraus
